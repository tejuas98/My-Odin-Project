chocolate ice cream

Description

Chocolate ice cream is a rich, creamy dessert made with a base of milk, sugar, and chocolate, often enhanced with egg yolks for a custard-like texture. It can be made with various methods, from traditional custard bases to no-churn versions, and is a popular treat for its indulgent flavor and smooth consistency


Ingredients

  • Heavy Cream: 1 to 3 cups (240 to 720 mL)
  • Whole Milk: 1 to 2 cups (240 to 480 mL)
  • Unsweetened Cocoa Powder: ¼ to 1 cup (25 to 120 g)
    • Use Dutch-process or dark cocoa for different flavor profiles
  • Granulated Sugar: ¾ to 1 cup (150 to 200 g)
    • Alternatives: cane sugar, light brown sugar, or sweetened condensed milk
  • Vanilla Extract: 1 to 2 teaspoons (5 to 10 mL)
  • Salt: ⅛ to ½ teaspoon (0.6 to 2.5 g), to enhance flavor
  • Chocolate (optional): 2 to 6 ounces (60 to 170 g) of chopped dark, semisweet, or milk chocolate, or chocolate chips
  • Egg Yolks (optional): 3 to 6 large yolks
    • Used in custard-based recipes for extra creaminess

Steps

  1. Prepare the Base:
    • Combine milk, cream, sugar, and cocoa powder in a saucepan.
    • Heat the mixture until it simmers and thickens, making sure the sugar and cocoa are fully dissolved.
    • Optional: For a custard base, whisk egg yolks with a portion of the hot mixture, then return it to the pan and cook gently until thickened.
  2. Incorporate Chocolate:
    • Remove the base from heat and stir in chopped chocolate until fully melted and smooth.
    • Optional no-cook method: Whisk cocoa powder, sugar, and milk until dissolved, then fold into whipped cream.
  3. Chill the Base:
    • Cool the mixture completely using an ice bath or by refrigerating for several hours.
    • This step is crucial for achieving the best texture in the final ice cream.
  4. Churn and Freeze:
    • Process the chilled base in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer's instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.
    • Transfer the churned ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for several hours until firm.
